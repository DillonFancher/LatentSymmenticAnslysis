Our Mission
We set out with a goal of using “Big Data” to create a platform that will aggregate Twitter data (“tweets”) into an interactive and visually appealing representation. In order to achieve this goal, we collected metadata from the Twitter fire hose using Twitter APIs. The metadata include: geolocation of the “tweet”, the grammatical content of the “tweet” itself, and the timestamp of the “tweet”. We are using a map of the Continental United States as our visual interface, and we have chosen to represent the prevalence of literacy (based on words in the “tweet”) in the U.S. with heat signatures.

A second goal of this project is to create an interactive representation. This was accomplished through our Twitter “EmotiMap” page, where users have the opportunity to select various options in the ultimate goal of creating a customized representation of a literacy map.

Finally, we set to make this map visually appealing. We have created a user flow that is easily navigable and inviting for users to enjoy their experience with our EmotiMap.

Our motivation for this project is to demonstrate how “Big Data” is relevant and meaningful to users of all backgrounds. We decided on literacy as our plotted measure, because we thought it would be fascinating to see where there are parts of the U.S. that use less words from the dictionary than other parts. It was also in our interest to see whether these changes are moderated by other factors, such as time. In an attempt to show the awesome power of “Big Data”, we thought Twitter to be a valid platform to access the literacy information. Through this project, we hope to demonstrate the effectiveness of our EmotiMap representation as a way to visualize literacy in the United States.
